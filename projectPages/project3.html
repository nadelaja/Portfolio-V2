About the Project

A Night at Home is an interactive narrative fiction game, also known as a choice-based or branching narrative game. This genre of storytelling allows readers to actively participate in shaping the narrative through their choices, creating a personalized journey through the story. Unlike linear fiction, where readers passively follow a predetermined path, branching narratives offer multiple potential storylines that diverge and converge based on reader decisions.

Version 2.0 Enhancements

A Night at Home 2.0 represents a significant evolution of the original Twine game, rebuilt as part of my graduate student project. Key improvements include:

  • A three-page structure featuring a welcoming home page, a gates page for quick navigation, and an immersive story page
  • Custom visual elements replacing standard Twine 2 animations
  • Enhanced navigation options throughout the experience
  • Save functionality (currently in debugging)
  • Planned audio implementation

Experience the Story

Alone at night in your quiet, empty house, a noise pulls you from your thoughts. Do you brush it off and try to sleep, or let curiosity pull you deeper into the shadows? A Night at Home is an interactive game that challenges players to navigate a maze of creeping dread and uneasy choices. As the house shifts around you, and strange events draw you toward something sinister, it's up to you to decide how far you'll go when fear gnaws at the edges of your sanity. Will you face the darkness, or will you turn away and hope it's only your imagination?

Ready to test your courage? Play A Night at Home and discover what lurks in the darkness of your own home.

Development Process & Playtesting

The development process was heavily informed by player feedback through multiple iterations:

Early Testing

Key feedback from early testing helped shape the narrative structure:

  • dentified issues with passage length, leading to more digestible segments
  • Suggested incorporating choices within the text rather than just at passage ends
  • Recommended varying the presentation of player options to maintain engagement

Later Testing

Further refinements based on additional testing:

  • Further text optimization for better pacing
  • Suggested implementing a "true bad ending" where the player is consumed by the dark entity
  • Confirmed appropriate gameplay duration (approximately 10 minutes per route)

Results & Impact

Playtesting showed players felt invested in their choices, with the familiar home setting deepening their connection. The branching narrative encouraged replayability as players explored different paths.

Challenges

Creating effective interactive horror meant balancing tension with meaningful choices, making sure each decision felt significant while maintaining a coherent narrative.

Passage Length and Pacing

Managing the length of passages was key to keeping the narrative moving smoothly. Through playtesting, I refined these sections to strike the right balance between atmosphere, context, and engagement, ensuring the pacing stayed consistent without overwhelming the reader.

Twine to Custom Implementation

The game was ported from Twine to a custom interface using Lazerwalker's Twison story format to export the narrative to JSON. This process, while challenging, gave me the freedom to create a more personalized and thematic user experience.

Custom UI Development

Developing a custom UI required balancing aesthetics and functionality. The interface was designed to enhance the horror atmosphere while remaining intuitive, featuring custom navigation, save functionality, and seamless visual transitions.
