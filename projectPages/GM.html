Green Mechanics Website Redesign

UX & Web Design / 2024 / Designer & Developer

Duration

9 Months
End-to-End UX Redesign

Objectives

Create a user-friendly website that showcases environmental services and helps potential clients quickly understand Green Mechanics' offerings.

Primary Goal

Enable visitors to determine fit in five clicks or less.

Role

  • UX Researcher
  • UI Designer
  • Frontend Developer

Impact & Outcomes

  • Revamped homepage and service structure for faster comprehension
  • Created high-fidelity Figma prototype for client handoff
  • Developed scalable design system for future site updates

To Understand the Problem Space

Our Team's Assumptions

  • Users struggled to understand offered services
  • Visual design lacked credibility
  • Content was overly technical
  • Navigation was confusing

Client Meeting

  • Emphasized showcasing impact, not just services
  • Wanted a professional, trust-building site

Competitive Analysis

  • Benchmarked 3 peer websites
  • Clear categories, client success stories, trust signals
  • Current site lacked consistent branding

User Interviews

  • 14 participants interviewed
  • Key pain points: confusing layout, unclear mission, missing proof of impact
  • Insights directly informed design decisions

Sprint Timeline

Research & Real Voices

User interviews and journey mapping grounded the IA redesign.

Ideas into Interfaces

Research Highlights

Content & Messaging Challenges

  • Technical jargon confused broader audiences
  • Mission and value statements were buried
  • Services lacked clear plain language descriptions

Trust & Credibility Gaps

  • Limited client stories, testimonials, or metrics
  • Inconsistent visual design and branding
  • Site lacked trust-building content

Navigation & Usability Pain Points

  • Difficulty locating specific services
  • Visual distractions reduced focus
  • Inconsistent navigation labels and structure

Artifacts Developed

Mapped the typical user journey to visualize expectations and identify friction points across key stages of engagement.

Quantified interview insights into a distribution chart to prioritize content strategy focus areas.

Design Priorities & Strategies

Building from our research, we developed clear design priorities across multiple focus areas using "How Might We" framing.

Design (UX Principles in Action)

1

Information Architecture & Navigation

  • Restructured navigation for clearer service discovery
  • Grouped offerings into intuitive categories
  • Simplified homepage for quick overview
2

Visual Hierarchy & Branding

  • Built consistent visual system (typography, colors, icons)
  • Prioritized trust-building content visually
  • Replaced stock images with real-world project visuals
3

Content Strategy & Messaging

  • Used plain language writing style
  • Clarified mission statements
  • Added project case studies and impact metrics
4

Accessibility & Usability

  • Optimized fully responsive layouts
  • Improved legibility, spacing, and visual clarity
  • Implemented accessibility standards

Reflection & Outcomes

  • Improved clarity for future users: Simplified complex offerings and made value more immediately visible.
  • Client-aligned solutions: Weekly collaboration led to actionable outcomes meeting business needs.
  • Strengthened design confidence: Deepened skills in IA, content strategy, and trust-building design.
  • Deliverables: Hi-fi prototype, content strategy, and design system delivered to client.

This project reinforced the power of user-centered design in simplifying complex information, building trust, and aligning design solutions with real organizational goals.

Lessons Learned

Communication is Key

  • Established weekly client check-ins
  • Created protocols for intra-team collaboration

Work Smarter, Not Harder

  • Adapted analysis process to efficiently manage interview data volume

Know Your Limits

  • Adjusted project scope to match timeline constraints
